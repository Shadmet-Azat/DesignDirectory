# Learned references — principles harvested from URLs the human flagged

This file **grows over time.** When the human sends a URL of a site they admire and says to remember it: scrape it
(extract-design / firecrawl-website-design-clone), then extract the **principle, not the surface** — *why* it
works, what idea is defended, what the signature move is (Quality Reference Filter). Append an entry below. If a
principle recurs across several references, consider promoting it into a `movements.md` entry or the macro-signal.

Keep entries about the *transferable principle*, never "copy this site." A reference is a target to exceed.

**Verify before you abstract:** EXERCISE the live interactions (hover, move the cursor across the hero, scroll every
section + backgrounds, drag, click, advance carousels) and enumerate every distinct component — don't infer a
behaviour from a static frame or a class name. Tag anything you couldn't drive `[inferred]`, never as fact; when the
human is available, confirm a capture manifest with them before writing. (An interaction read statically is how a
cursor-reveal gets logged as a "glitch," or a live background/carousel gets missed — see Lando Norris, Session 30.)

## Entry template
```
### <short name> — <url> (added <date>)
- Movement / closest fit: <which of the core-13, or "new">

- Signature move: <the memorable mechanic / treatment — and why it works>

- Cliché it avoids: <what a generic version would have done instead>
```

### Relats "Top Tier" — https://toptier.relats.com/ (added 2026-07-02)
- Movement / closest fit: Cinematic/scrollytelling (#11) × Luxury (#9) applied to a Technical/industrial (#10) subject — "industrial product cinema."
- The defended idea: an unglamorous B2B commodity (textile cable-protection sleeves / wire loom) is treated as a luxury object — the product's OWN material, shot as hyper-macro cinematic render, IS the entire aesthetic, and the orange sleeve is the through-line that threads every market scene (EV busbar → excavator → truck → railway → agriculture). "Look closely enough and wire loom is beautiful."
- Signature move: a SINGLE dataset shipped as TWO synchronized views — **"Full view"** (a long cinematic scroll through per-market render scenes, each its own palette/lighting world, with real spec panels) and **"Overview"** (a warm bento dashboard of the same products/specs/industries at `/overview/`). The visitor chooses immersion or efficiency. Works because it resolves the immersion-vs-usability tension instead of picking a side: the brand gets its film, the engineer/buyer gets the facts fast.
- Worth stealing (the principle): (1) **CATEGORY TRANSPOSITION** — apply the top-tier treatment of a glamorous category to an unglamorous one; the mismatch itself is the wow. (2) **DUAL-VIEW escape hatch** — a cinematic experience earns its indulgence by offering a parallel efficient view of the identical content. (3) **real information design** — every spec panel carries TRUE data (`+1000°C×5min ≥1.5kV (TE)`; `-70→+225°C, peaks +300°C`; icons map to real protective properties) → passes the meaning gate's key test. (4) craft stack: **Roobert** (grotesque, only weights 400/500) + **DM Mono** (the spec/technical voice) on **warm neutrals** (`#eae4df` greige / `#faf9f8` off-white) + near-black `#0a0a0a` + ONE constant signature orange `#ff5710` (the product's own color), with per-scene situational colors (electric blue `#3131ff`, green `#42ec8b`); a **NAMED easing vocabulary as CSS vars** (`expo.out 0.16,1,0.3,1` · `expo.inOut 0.87,0,0.13,1` · a custom slight-overshoot `0.05,0.76,0.38,1.015`); Lenis + GSAP ScrollTrigger; pre-rendered muted looping video scenes (poster-backed, autoplay-on-scroll).
- Colour + aesthetic harmony (how it all looks TOGETHER — the human's flagged concern, not just the hex list): the accent `#ff5710` is the *product's own orange*, so UI and imagery share one hue → total cohesion. It sits on WARM neutrals (`#eae4df` greige · `#faf9f8` off-white · `#d3cac3` taupe), the SAME temperature as the accent, so orange belongs to the ground instead of clashing (a cold grey would fight it). Orange is held RARE (CTAs · active nav pill · key specs · the product itself) while **VALUE contrast** (near-black `#0a0a0a` page ↔ warm-white tiles) does the heavy lifting — low hue-variety + high value-contrast reads sophisticated; many saturated hues reads AI-slop. Secondary colours are **scene-scoped**: electric blue `#3131ff` and green `#42ec8b` live only inside their act, never in the global chrome — and blue is orange's near-complement, so the orange-sleeve-on-blue EV scene is the punchiest (classic cinematic orange/blue). Type pairing carries the same duality: **Roobert** (humanist grotesque = warm, brand; only weights 400/500) for headlines + **DM Mono** (geometric mono = precise, engineering) for eyebrows/specs — "warm brand meets hard engineering." Fluid clamp radius + generous gaps + warm grain keep dense technical content calm and tactile. The whole aesthetic = warm-minimal × cinematic × technical, fused into one coherent world.
- The Overview page — why "everything in one place" works (the human's flagged favourite): it is a TRUE single-screen dashboard (`scrollHeight === viewport`, zero scroll) — a 3-column asymmetric flex masonry of **typed** boxes (`--text` hero · `--slider` product+live specs · `--video` with a kinetic spec number · `--industries` list · `--sustainability` photo · `--carousel` colorways), heights tuned so all columns bottom-align to the fold. The trick that fits "everything" without clutter: **density through TIME, not space** — any box holding more than fits is its own internal carousel (~25 on the page) cycling products/specs/colours in place, so nothing is dropped and nothing scrolls. The whole catalogue is graspable in one frame, and stays calm because a strict column grid disciplines the abundance (maximal surface over rigorous structure).
- Cliché it avoids: the default B2B-industrial site (stock factory photos, spec PDFs, cold grey `#f5f5f5`, Inter, a "Request a quote" form) — and equally the opposite trap of an immersive scroll with NO efficient path for the buyer who just needs the datasheet.
- QRF verdict: **QUALITY REFERENCE for its CONCEPT + ARCHITECTURE** (category transposition, dual-view, real info-design). Its techniques (Lenis + ScrollTrigger + video scenes) are current-standard award craft executed very well, not novel.
- Exceed-target: replace passive video-*watching* with **real-time interactive exploration** (spin the sleeve, live colorway swap, hover a 3D part to reveal its spec) so the user EXPLORES instead of watches; and tighten the Full-view pacing (a ~135k-px scroll risks fatigue even with the Overview safety valve).

### Lando Norris — https://landonorris.com/ (added 2026-07-02) — by OFF+BRAND
- Movement / closest fit: 3D-immersive/spatial (#12) **re-aimed at a PERSON** (personal-brand / identity, not a spatial product) × Editorial-magazine (#2) sports photography × bold display type — "**the athlete as a living interface.**"
- The defended idea: a driver's identity is **DUAL** — **ON TRACK** (the machine-self: helmet, results, trackside) vs **OFF TRACK** (the human: campaigns, dogs, black-tie) — and the *whole site is built on that split*. It's the thesis line ("Defining a legacy in Formula 1 *on and off the track*"), it gets a dedicated confrontation section, and it IS the primary nav (On Track / Off Track are the two doors + the footer's top links). The site doesn't *describe* the duality; its architecture **is** the duality.
- Signature move: the **OTOT split** — Lando's lime helmet in profile (ON TRACK) faces his bare human profile (OFF TRACK) across the centre, mirrored, confronting each other, a hand-scripted lime "ON" laid over block-serif "OFF / TRACK" and one lime door into each world (the helmet's blob livery even rhymes with his off-track fleece pattern). And the **hero is a CURSOR-DRIVEN REVEAL** (verified live): moving the pointer paints away the bare-skin layer to expose his *real painted helmet livery* beneath (Monster claw, papaya swoosh, Quadrant blob, chrome visor) in **soft liquid-diffusion patches that trace the cursor**, over a constant ghosted **3D-helmet wireframe** armature — so **you literally uncover the racer under the person with your own hand.** The duality isn't just shown, it's made *tactile and interactive*: the visitor performs the on-track/off-track reveal. Both the split and the hero pass the meaning gate hard (key + swap tests: move either to another driver and it collapses — nothing here is generic decoration).
- Worth stealing (the principle): (1) **make the medium REAL-TIME INTERACTIVE, not video.** Nearly every mark — logo, hamburger, buttons, the interactive circuit map, the animating *signature*, the *recolorable* helmet — is a **Rive state machine** reacting to scroll/hover, with **colour inputs** that recolor per section, plus a genuine WebGL 3D helmet. This is the *concrete answer to Relats' logged exceed-target* ("replace passive video-watching with real-time interactive exploration"): aliveness + agency at a fraction of video's weight. The full real-time kit (verified live): the cursor-reveal hero, a **scroll-driven concentric-oval background** (`data-oval-scroll`, clip-revealed line rings that drift as you scroll — the "moving circles" motif), a **WebGL carousel** (`gl-carousel`, GPU transitions not a plain scroll-snap), the Rive circuit map, buttons and signature — every layer real-time, none a flat video. (2) **a defended DUALITY can BE the information architecture** — sibling to Relats' dual-VIEW (there the split was immersion vs efficiency; here it's identity: on/off track). One defended split, carried through the whole site, is a repeatable top-tier move. (3) **fuse the two halves in ONE hero image** (human face + machine skin) so the concept lands before a single scroll. (4) a **living-identity system**: one interactive-vector runtime drives logo + UI + helmet + signature as a coherent reactive family that recolors with a per-section `theme`.
- Colour + aesthetic harmony (incl. the imagery — the human's flagged concern): a **green-tinted warm-neutral** world — the darks are olive-green (`--color--dark-green #282c20`), *not* black, and even the greys/whites are green-cast (`green-off-white #dde1d2` · `grey-on-track #b9bbad` · `cream #efefe5`) — so the whole ground reads warm-organic-sport, never sterile tech-white. On it sits ONE electric signature: **lime `#d2ff00`** (confirmed *from the pixels* — the helmet livery samples `#d0f010`), held rare (STORE button, the script "ON", the nav doors, the L7 mark). Second accent = McLaren **papaya `#ff6b00`**, the team tie-in, rarer still. Photography is warm and lightly desaturated so the lime is the only thing that truly sings — **low hue-variety + high value contrast** (olive-dark ↔ green-cream), the same sophistication discipline Relats used; off-track campaign shots introduce warmer editorial tones (crimson/rose ~`#d03050`) but only *inside their own scenes*, never in the global chrome. Type carries the duality too: **Brier** (a heavy condensed display) for impact — the "LANDO NORRIS" wordmark + the giant "ON/OFF TRACK" — against **Mona Sans Variable** (clean humanist grotesque) for everything else. Brutal-bold display vs neutral workhorse = the same "two-voices" pairing logic as Relats' Roobert × DM Mono, tuned for sport not engineering.
- QRF verdict: **QUALITY REFERENCE for TECHNIQUE** (real-time interactive-vector systems via Rive + a depth-mapped 2.5D/3D identity hero) **and for CONCEPT** (a meaning-locked identity duality that *is* the architecture). It is the concrete realisation of [[Relats]]'s exceed-target. **Current-standard** (competent, not novel) for its scaffolding — the pinned horizontal-scroll career gallery, per-section theme-switching, marquees.
- Exceed-target: (1) **resolve the hero's legibility** — the human+machine composite is meaningful but reads noisy/unresolved in a still (it can tip from "fusion" to "smear"); make the two states legible and clearly intentional, distorting only on interaction. (2) **deepen the agency** — the *hero* already gives genuine pointer agency (you paint the reveal yourself), but the deeper objects (the 3D helmet, the circuit map) are still mostly scroll/hover-*triggered* canned states rather than freely explorable; let the user actually spin/recolor the helmet and drive the map at will (the interactivity is present; the deep *exploration* isn't yet). (3) **tighten pacing + add a light path** — ~24.6k px / ~12 screens with a 7.5k-px pinned horizontal track is long, and 21 canvases + WebGL + 8 Rive files + DRACO is heavy: ship a genuine reduced-motion / low-power fallback.

### Vanity Studio — https://www.vanity.llc/ (added 2026-07-03)
- Movement / closest fit: agency/studio dark **portfolio-showcase**; closest to 3D-immersive/spatial (#12) **but built in PURE CSS, not WebGL** — the lightweight *floor* of that movement (Webflow + GSAP).
- The defended idea: "**Design that sells**" — the studio proves its craft by making its *own portfolio the demo*: the client work-samples themselves (Dior, Chanel, Porsche, Miami Estates…) ARE the hero, choreographed in 3D space rather than described. **Honest caveat:** the idea is thin — it's a showcase, not a defended concept; the *choreography* is the value, and it does not MEAN anything specific about the studio (see QRF/meaning note below).
- Signature move: a **scroll-scrubbed 2D-ring → 3D-carousel morph of photo cards.** At rest the 8 project cards sit as a flat tilted RING orbiting the centred "Design that sells." headline (each a 2D `matrix()` ~45° apart, verified by decoding the live transforms); as you scroll, ONE master timeline flips them to `matrix3d` (rotateY + translateZ + translateY) so the flat ring **unfolds into an overlapping fanned 3D deck** that sweeps across and recedes into depth, then clears to reveal the Services section (screenshot-grounded at scroll 0 / 1150 / 4088). Why it works: it earns genuine 3D depth + a satisfying state-change reveal from **pure CSS transforms under one `perspective: 1400px`** — no canvas, no WebGL — the lightweight inverse of [[Lando Norris]]'s 21-canvas rig for a comparable "3D photographic carousel" outcome.
- Worth stealing (the principle): (1) **award-tier 3D photo motion with ZERO WebGL** — `perspective` + `matrix3d` on plain `<img>` wrappers + ONE light-scrub ScrollTrigger (`scrub: 0.12`) buys real depth cheaply and degrades gracefully; reach for this before a Three.js scene when the "3D" is a card *carousel*, not a lit model. (2) **a `position: sticky` + 100vh stage, NOT a GSAP `pin: true`** — the cards stay centred while a tall spacer section scrolls past; cheaper, more accessible, no pin-spacer reflow (verified: `pin:false`, no `.pin-spacer`, one sticky perspective stage). (3) **make the showpiece CONVERT** — the cards click through to the actual projects (user-confirmed), so the wow isn't a dead end. (4) the morph is a **state-change** (flat ring → 3D deck), not a spin — a change of ARRANGEMENT reads as more designed than a rotation.
- Colour + harmony: a **pure-black void** where the full-bleed project imagery supplies ALL the colour (monochrome ground + a system sans; a live "Miami, FL · 5:2x PM" clock + a section-swapping `/vanity studio`→`/services` breadcrumb are the only chrome). Deliberately lets the WORK be the palette — the inverse of Relats/Lando's "one subject-accent on same-temperature neutrals" (this is NOT that pattern; logged honestly — no 3rd instance).
- Cliché it avoids: a flat 2D grid of portfolio thumbnails — OR the opposite trap, a heavy Three.js/WebGL scene for what CSS 3D does at a fraction of the weight.
- QRF verdict: **QUALITY REFERENCE for the LIGHTWEIGHT technique** (a genuine CSS-only 3D photo carousel + sticky-stage-not-pin) and for one nice idea (the ring→carousel *morph*). **Current-standard** for the rest (scroll-driven 3D card decks are known award craft). **CAUTIONARY on MEANING** — the choreography is beautiful decoration that encodes nothing true about the studio; it *fails the swap test* (drop any other studio's images in and it's unchanged). This is the first logged **split-verdict reference: high CRAFT, low MEANING** — a concrete negative example for the meaning gate (motion ≠ meaning). Cross-ref: the technical inverse of [[Lando Norris]] (heavy WebGL, deep meaning) — same 3D-carousel family, opposite ends of BOTH the weight axis and the meaning axis.
- Exceed-target: (1) **tie the choreography to a real idea** so it passes the meaning gate — e.g. scattered client brands *assembling into one unified system*, or cards building a portfolio wall — right now the ring→deck is arbitrary. (2) ship a **reduced-motion / low-power** fallback (one master scrub with no static end-state = nothing for reduced-motion users). (3) the cards already click through (good) — deepen to a hover-preview or a filterable wall so exploration matches the craft.

### Active Theory — https://activetheory.net/ (added 2026-07-03)
- Movement / closest fit: **3D-immersive/spatial (#12) at its absolute CEILING** × Generative/creative-coding (#13) — "**the studio site as a real-time engine: the whole page is ONE GPU application, not a document.**" This is the reference other WebGL studios are measured against.
- The defended idea: Active Theory doesn't *claim* to be the most technically capable experiential studio — **the site IS the proof.** The entire portfolio runs on their proprietary **Hydra** engine inside a *single WebGL canvas* (verified live: the DOM is literally `<div id="Stage">` + one `<canvas>` — **0 `<img>`, exactly 1 CSS variable, 57 chars of body text**; everything — text via MSDF `GLText`, the whole UI via a WebGL toolkit `GLUI`, icons, cursor, transitions — is GPU-rendered). The medium *is* the message: "we build worlds in a browser; watch." Crucially they re-implement the boring-hard web contract *inside* the GL layer — **`GLSEO` + `GLA11y`** build a real crawlable/screen-reader tree for a canvas that has no DOM (snapshot-confirmed: Work/Contact/song controls are keyboard-navigable) — so the flex doesn't abandon accessibility.
- Signature move (the persistent lens + the spine + **the cards**): the one constant object is a **refractive glass "a" logo** that behaves as a **LENS** — the showreel and scene worlds play *through* it (chromatic dispersion on the ring; it rotates/morphs on scroll, `HomeLogoShader`/`Home/refraction`), bookended by a **glass ribbon** that descends at the opening and **loops closed into a teardrop** at the end (the journey returns to origin). Then WORK: a giant **iridescent 3D SPINE** (`spine.bin`, oil-slick nacre) is the *literal backbone of the portfolio* and **project cards branch off the vertebrae** — and the cards are the star (the human's flagged discovery, "at first I didn't even notice them"): **each card is a refractive lens at rest that RESOLVES to an embedded video / live graphic on hover, and CLICKS THROUGH into a fully cursor-interactive project scene** (each card unique, deep-linked e.g. `/work/echo`, with year / client / category + description + PROJECT LINK / CLOSE). Project **titles are 3D extruded/MSDF type** that moves with scroll and hits a **chromatic RGB-split distortion** on transition. After the spine: **"THE LAB"** — a surface of **interactive hexagonal SCALES** (fish-scale / chainmail, `hexgrid`+`chainlink` geometry) reacting to the pointer, under an underwater caustic. Plus real navigation-as-experience extras (all verified in the running app's live state): an **"ASK ME ANYTHING" AI assistant** to find work (`InteractAIAssistant`), **QR-code mobile multiplayer** (phone as controller / 2nd player, `MobileSync` + hand-tracking inputs), and a **music player** (tracks like "SELF — DUSK"), plus a **scroll-reactive nav indicator** (a mark that morphs/slides between WORK|CONTACT with scroll position + direction). Why it works: the whole site is a *playable world* in which the studio's capability is **demonstrated, not asserted** — and every signature (lens-logo, spine-backbone, hover-resolve cards) is meaning-locked to "a portfolio of real-time experiences."
- Worth stealing (the principle): (1) **when the CRAFT itself is the product, the site can be a pure demonstration of capability** — build the thing you sell. This is the honest, *earned* twin of [[Relats]]'s category-transposition and the exact inverse of [[Vanity Studio]]'s empty decoration: Vanity's spectacle failed the swap test; AT's spectacle passes because spectacle literally *is* the service. (2) **one persistent refractive "hero object" as a LENS / throughline** the whole journey passes through (you see the reel *through* the logo; the ribbon opens and closes the loop) — a single carried object beats a fresh hero per section. (3) **the portfolio card as a refractive lens that RESOLVES on hover and ENTERS on click** — a card that hides its content behind glass, rewards the pointer by resolving to live video, then clicks into an interactive scene → *browsing becomes discovery.* Reusable for any high-craft portfolio. (4) **a literal STRUCTURAL METAPHOR for the collection** — the spine as the backbone the projects hang off (a shape that *means* "these are the vertebrae of our body of work"). (5) **if you go full-canvas, you OWE the web its contract back** — a real a11y tree + crawlable text (`GLA11y`/`GLSEO`) is the discipline that separates a responsible showpiece from an inaccessible flex.
- Colour + aesthetic harmony (incl. imagery — the human's flagged concern): a **deep-sea / bioluminescent** world. Pure-black base washed with **deep teal-cyan radial gradients** (near-black ~`#050d0e` → teal glow ~`#1b3d3a`/`#2f6f6b`), god-rays, volumetric dust — a cool blue-green ground that **"breathes"** (the ambient environment colour drifts teal↔green over time / with the pointer, IBL-driven). The signature accent is **NOT a pigment — it's OPTICAL**: full-spectrum **chromatic dispersion** (the rainbow refraction on the glass logo/ring) + **iridescent nacre** (the oil-slick spine, purple/cyan/magenta). UI chrome stays strictly **monochrome** (white text on a near-black pill), so *all* colour comes from **materials** (glass, iridescence) and **project imagery** (Secret Sky greens, IBM Harmonic State corals, work-scene purples) — the same "low-hue-variety chrome, let content/material carry colour" discipline as [[Relats]]/[[Lando Norris]], except here the "accent" is a *physical light phenomenon* rather than a brand hue. **One type voice only: NB Architekt** (Neubau — a technical/architectural mono-grotesque) at 300/400/700, all-caps and letterspaced — a single engineering voice for a studio whose product is engineering (no display×body pairing; the imagery is the second voice).
- Cliché it avoids: the agency-portfolio default (a grid of case-study thumbnails + hover-zoom + a big-type manifesto on white) — AND the trap most WebGL showreels fall into that AT specifically doesn't: **spectacle with no web contract** (no SEO, no screen-reader tree, no keyboard path). AT keeps `GLSEO`/`GLA11y` even inside the flex — though its real remaining gap is the opposite end (heavy weight, no low-power path).
- QRF verdict: **QUALITY REFERENCE — the CEILING itself for TECHNIQUE/craft** (a proprietary cross-platform engine — `GLUI`, MSDF `GLText`, GL-native `GLSEO`/`GLA11y`, an 8-thread `hydra-thread` worker pool, a DRACO + Basis/KTX2 + RGBM-HDR + baked-Cycles-lighting + LUT pipeline — the whole site as one GPU app). This is the **apex of movement #12**. On CONCEPT it is a **split verdict like [[Vanity Studio]] but INVERTED**: Vanity = high-craft / low-meaning decoration; AT = high-craft *and* the craft honestly *is* the message (medium = message passes cleanly for a studio whose product IS the medium) — yet the identity narrative ("we are the most capable") is true and earned but **not as transferable a STORY** as Relats' real info-design or Lando's identity-duality. So: **quality-reference for HOW, a narrower lesson for WHAT.**
- Exceed-target: the whole experience is **heavy and long** — a **47,646-px / ~23-viewport** virtual journey (driven live via its `.FXScroll` proxy → a normalized `ViewController/scrollV` across router scenes home→work→footer) on top of a full engine + DRACO + KTX2 + an 8-worker pool, with **no visible low-power / reduced-motion path**. The transferable win: **this level of world-building AND the genuinely novel ideas (one-canvas-everything, GL-native a11y, AI-assistant-as-navigation, refractive-lens cards, spine-as-collection) delivered at a fraction of the weight, with a real reduced-motion / low-power fallback and a shorter spine** — keep the wonder, lose the 30-second load and the fatigue. (Cross-ref the weight axis: [[Vanity Studio]] proved a "3D card carousel" can be pure-CSS and near-weightless; AT is the maximal opposite pole — the reusable target sits between them. **[[Samana Studio]] (Entry #5) is the concrete occupant of that middle** — a real WebGL world at ~½ AT's weight, and meaning-locked where Vanity was empty.)

### Samana Studio — https://samanastudio.es/ (added 2026-07-03)
- Movement / closest fit: **3D-immersive/spatial (#12)** — but the **meaning-locked, mid-weight** occupant of that movement, not the apex: a real WebGL world (one canvas, one bundle) that deliberately sits *between* [[Vanity Studio]] (near-weightless pure-CSS, empty) and [[Active Theory]] (maximal one-canvas engine). "**The studio's pitch, walked — a descent into depth that literally embodies 'immersion.'**"
- The defended idea: a boutique studio selling "immersive, cinematic, WebGL websites — motion, depth and light that make your brand *felt, not just seen*" proves it by making the site a **literal descent into the deep**. Scroll = diving down; a **depth meter counts the metres** (—000 M → ~—280 M); the manifesto ("atmosphere → presence → emergence") is delivered as *stations you pass in the dark*, each a light-filled form (faceted crystal monoliths glowing from within → a translucent bioluminescent koi). The medium is the argument: you can't *describe* "felt not seen," so the site makes you feel depth, atmosphere and light before it makes a claim. Passes the meaning gate hard — the descent is the studio's exact thesis made spatial (swap test: this metaphor collapses for a non-immersive studio).
- Signature move — **TWO coupled navigation axes** (the memorable mechanic): (1) **scroll = descent**, quantified by a **depth-meter HUD in real metres** (eased/momentum counter, not a progress bar) — the vertical spine; and (2) **click a glowing waypoint = a lateral dive-IN** — the manifesto "points of interest" are **3D-anchored HUD markers** (halo/ring/dot buttons projected from points *on* the crystal to 2-D screen space, active-by-proximity, keyboard-focusable) and **clicking one dollies the camera right up to that point on the object and opens a mono text-card** explaining that beat ("A page informs. An experience moves…"), decoupled from scroll depth. So **scroll goes deeper, clicking goes closer** — a genuinely two-axis spatial read of a one-page site. Why it works: the depth meter turns a scroll into a *dive* (wayfinding + drama in one HUD element), and the clickable waypoints give real *agency* inside the scene (you choose which presence to approach) without a heavy portfolio — exactly the "let the user EXPLORE, not watch" agency [[Relats]] asked for, delivered at a fraction of [[Active Theory]]'s weight.
- Worth stealing (the principle): (1) **the site's core METAPHOR is a spatial journey that embodies the pitch** — when you sell an intangible (immersion, depth, calm, speed), build the literal spatial experience of it rather than asserting it; the metaphor is the proof. (2) **quantify the journey as wayfinding + drama** — a real-unit readout (metres of depth) is better than a progress bar: it *means* something in-world and raises the stakes as the number climbs. (3) **two-axis navigation** — a vertical spine (scroll) crossed with an in-scene focus axis (click-to-dolly on anchored POIs) gives a small site real explorable depth without more pages. (4) **light as the only colour** — subsurface-scattering forms lit *from within* + volumetric god-rays make "light makes your brand felt" a literal rendering choice (see harmony). (5) **mid-weight is a real target** — you do NOT need AT's engine to be meaning-locked and beautiful: one `<canvas>`, one bundle, Geist, ~20.7k px clears the bar.
- Colour + aesthetic harmony (brief — token route not taken, but the entry always logs it): a **monochrome deep-sea** world — near-black blue base (~`#050a12`) that **drifts to deep teal-green with depth** (the bioluminescent band), UI chrome strictly monochrome (Geist, white/greige), and ONE cool accent `--frost:#9fd0ff` (CTA rim-glow, HUD halos). As with [[Active Theory]], **the "colour" is really LIGHT, not pigment**: the warm-gold glow *inside* the crystals and the cyan glow *inside* the koi (subsurface scattering) + white marine-snow particulate + god-ray shafts carry all the warmth; the palette itself is one-hue.
